Sorcerer's Void

Sorcerer's Void is a third-person action RPG inspired by iconic Souls-like titles such as Elden Ring, Black Myth: Wukong, and Lies of P. This game was developed using Unreal Engine as part of a Senior Capstone Project at the University of Nevada, Reno, in collaboration with two other team members. Staying true to the Souls-like genre, Sorcerer's Void incorporates key features such as fluid player controls, unique enemies, a progression system, and immersive storytelling.

As a team of three guys who grew up playing video games, our goal was to create a game we could proudly showcase to faculty, friends, family, and potential employers. We chose the Souls-like genre because it demands a careful balance between fun and challenge, a critical factor in the success of these games. Achieving this balance demonstrates a solid understanding of what it takes to craft an engaging and rewarding gaming experience.

This page highlights the parts of the project I contributed to, including:
    Player Controls: Developing core gameplay mechanics.
    Checkpoint System: Implementing a save-and-respawn feature.
    Tutorial System: Creating a user-friendly onboarding experience for new players.

Gameplay Features

Tutorial

The tutorial area is designed to help familiarize the player with game controls and mechanics.

Weapons

Selection of weapon types with unique combat styles, enhancing gameplay variety.

Blocking

Blocking allows player to reduce incoming attack damage at a cost of stamina or fully deflect all damage with perfectly timed parries.

Execution

Enemies can be dismembered during combat, creating cinematic execution moments that enhance immersion.

Healing

Healing is a scarce resource that can be used only a few times. Once fully depleted, the player can regain additional uses through combat.

Checkpoint

Players can save their progress and respawn at designated checkpoints scattered throughout the game world.
